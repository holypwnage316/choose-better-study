The Great Warfare

Beneath everything else recorded in biblical history is a war — between Satan, God's rebellious creature, and God himself.

Satan

Malevolent — attempting to destroy all true pleasure, health, happiness, and holiness

God

Benevolent — ultimately establishing in his created order the richest pleasure, health, happiness, and holiness

Lord Sabaoth

One of the most common titles for God in the Bible — used 259 times — is Lord Sabaoth: the Lord of Hosts, or the Lord of Armies.

The title refers to God as he wages terrifying (and ultimately triumphant) war against his enemies and the enemies of his people.

God's warfare in geopolitical Israel is a type of his eschatological warfare — anticipated in Genesis 3 and consummated in the book of Revelation.

Biblical Basis

The warfare model draws from three streams of Scripture:

1. Old Testament Typology

Expectations of Christ often take the form of a military king leading God's people to victory

2. Apocalyptic Passages

The whole drama of human history framed as a war between good and evil, light and darkness

3. Military Language in the NT

Our weapons, our armor, our enemies, our internal conflicts (2 Cor. 10:3–4; Eph. 6:11–13; James 4:1; 1 Peter 2:11)

Apocalyptic Warfare

Scripture's vision of the end of history is consistently military:

  • God as conquering warrior restoring order, justice, and peace (Rev. 19:11–21)
  • Michael and his angels fighting the dragon in heaven (Rev. 12:7)
  • The beast waging war against the Lamb (Rev. 17)
  • Christ threatening war against false teaching within the churches (Rev. 2)

These visions make clear: there is no neutrality. Righteousness and wickedness, submission to God and resistance to his reign, are utterly incompatible.

Military Language in the NT

Paul calls ministers soldiers and charges Timothy to "wage the good warfare" (1 Tim. 1:18). Jesus describes the church as a force breaking open the prison walls of Hades — an image of assault, not defense.

Ephesians 6:11–13

"Put on the whole armor of God... For we do not wrestle against flesh and blood, but against the rulers, against the authorities, against the cosmic powers over this present darkness... having done all, to stand firm."

How the Warfare Model Functions

Crucial caveat: None of this is technique. Progress in spiritual warfare depends entirely on Christ — he alone has defeated the Serpent. We engage this warfare wholly dependent on the Spirit.

1. Watchfulness

"Be sober-minded; be watchful. Your adversary the devil prowls around like a roaring lion, seeking someone to devour." (1 Peter 5:8)

Obedience & Equipment

2. Obedience to Orders

The battlefield is not the place for debate — orders are followed. The warfare model includes everything the law model includes: soldiers must obey. (Matt. 8:8–10)

3. Equipment and Preparation

An ill-equipped army loses regardless of courage — many battles are won before the first shot is fired. Scripture calls believers to the same seriousness: the armor of God (Eph. 6) and weapons with divine power (2 Cor. 10:3–6).

Strategy & Knowledge

4. Strategy — Offensive and Defensive

Where can we advance and take ground? Where are we most vulnerable? Believers need long-range plans to make progress and honest assessments of where the Enemy might exploit weaknesses.

5. Knowledge of the Enemy

"...so that we would not be outwitted by Satan; for we are not ignorant of his designs." (2 Cor. 2:10–11)

Are We Ignorant of His Designs?

We know Satan tries to break our communion with God and tempt us to sin — but do we know how he goes about either?

Consider our high-tech culture: what Satan desires most is to disrupt our communion with God, and a world of constant social media, texting, and notifications makes uninterrupted prayer and meditation nearly impossible.

Satan's Weapons: Deceit

Scripture consistently reveals two primary weapons: deceit and desire.

"He was a murderer from the beginning, and does not stand in the truth, because there is no truth in him. When he lies, he speaks out of his own character, for he is a liar and the father of lies." (John 8:44)

His original deception — that God is malevolent and following him is wiser than obeying God — remains his primary strategy. As Thomas Brooks wrote: Satan's great device is to present the bait and hide the hook.

Cultural Habit & Diversion

Cultural Habit

Every culture creates "plausibility structures" — making some values seem normal and others seem strange. Since all cultures are infected by human rebellion, what feels normal may be far from what God reveals as true.

Diversion

When Satan cannot convince us that good is evil, he diverts our energy. He would rather the church fight herself than fight him — and cultivates appetite for entertainment over the means of grace.

Discussion Question

Desire, Faith & Repentance

Satan's Weapon: Desire

Because of the fall, our desires are upside-down — we want the creation more than the Creator. These desires can grow so strong the Puritans called them "violent." Apart from God's grace, they can feel completely irresistible. (James 4:1–2; 1 Peter 2:11)

Our Weapons: Faith and Repentance

Faith counters deceit — the more our minds are shaped by Scripture, the harder it is to deceive us. Repentance counters desire — a soul-felt recognition of how vile and deadly sin is.

Faith and repentance are like a soldier's two legs: with one we stride toward God, with the other we stride away from sin.

Discussion Question

Application: Mary & Bill

Their church needs someone to chair a new ministry. Both consider it. The warfare model gives them different answers.

Mary — Decline

Already overextended. Little time for prayer or meditation. She needs contemplative space, not more activity.

Bill — Accept

Single and withdrawn. Active service would develop areas where he is weak — taking new ground from the Enemy.

Same question. Two legitimate answers.

Key Takeaways

1. Beneath all of history is a real war — between God and Satan, good and evil, life and death

2. Every decision we make either serves the forces of good or the forces of evil

3. The Christian life requires watchfulness, obedience, preparation, strategy, and knowledge of the Enemy

4. Satan's primary weapons are deceit and desire; ours are faith and repentance

5. Victory is certain — Christ has already crushed the Serpent — but vigilance is still required
